# Peacock Investment Club

### *Many feathers, one fortune.*

A simple, friendly guide to what Peacock Investment Club is and how it works — written for
everyone, with no technical words. If you've never seen the app and just want to understand the
idea and each feature, start here.

---

## What is Peacock Investment Club?

Peacock Investment Club is a way for a group of friends to **save money together, help each other
with loans, and grow their money as a team** — and to keep all of it fair and out in the open.

The name says it: like a peacock's many feathers making one beautiful bird, **many members'
small monthly savings come together into one shared fortune.**

In short, the club does three things:

1. **Everyone saves a little every month.**
2. **The club lends that pooled money to members who need it**, and they pay a small interest.
3. **The club also grows the money outside** (for example in a bank or a chit fund).

All the earnings belong to the members, shared fairly.

---

## The people in the club

Everyone is a **member**. Some members simply take on an extra helping role:

- **Member** — anyone in the club. They save every month, can borrow when they need to, and can
  leave and come back later.
- **Money-holder (treasurer)** — a member who is **looking after some of the club's cash** for a
  while. Anyone can do this, and more than one person can hold money at the same time.
- **Manager (admin)** — a member who is allowed to **keep the records** (note down who paid, who
  borrowed, and so on).

Nobody is "above" anyone else. The manager and money-holder roles are just helping hands — **all
members are equal**.

---

## Where is the club's money kept?

Here's something important and a little unusual: **the club itself doesn't have a bank account or
a cash box.** The club isn't a building or an organisation with its own locker.

Instead, **the club's money is kept safely by trusted members** (the money-holders). So at any
time, the club's total cash is simply **everything those members are holding, added together**.

- When you pay your monthly savings, you hand it to one of the money-holders.
- When someone gets a loan, the cash comes from one of the money-holders.
- Money-holders can pass cash to each other when needed.

The app always shows **who is holding how much**, so nothing is ever lost track of.

## The features, one by one

### 1. Monthly savings

Every member puts in a **fixed amount each month**. Today that amount is **₹2,000** (it used to be
₹1,000 in the club's early years and was raised later). The app keeps track of:

- how much each member **should** have paid by now, and
- how much they **actually** paid.

If you're behind, the app gently shows it as **pending** so you can catch up.

### 2. Joining later — the "catch-up"

The club has been running for years and has already grown. So if **a new person joins now**, it
wouldn't be fair for them to instantly share in years of growth they didn't help build.

To make it fair, a new member pays a one-time **catch-up**: roughly the savings they "missed" plus
their share of the profit the club has already earned. After that, **they're equal to everyone
else**. The app works out a suggested amount, and the manager can adjust it.

The same idea helps a member who fell behind on monthly savings catch back up.

### 3. Borrowing money (loans)

Members can borrow from the club's pooled money. The simple rules:

- You can borrow **up to ₹5,00,000** (this limit can be changed).
- You can have **only one loan at a time** — clear it before taking another.
- A loan should be paid back **within 5 months**.
- After you finish a loan, there's a **one-month wait** before borrowing again.
- You can **repay any amount at any time** — a little or all of it.

Sometimes the full loan is handed over **in parts**, because one money-holder may not have all the
cash at once. For example, you might get part of your loan today and the rest a few days later.
That's all treated as **one loan**.

### 4. How interest works (the cost of a loan)

When you borrow, you pay a small **interest** — today **1% per month** on what you owe.

It's counted in a fair, easy-to-picture way:

- For **each full month** you keep the loan, you pay that month's 1%.
- For the **extra days** beyond the last full month, you pay a small per-day amount — one
  thirtieth of the monthly interest per day, whichever month it is.

And here's the fair part: **as you repay, interest is charged only on what's still left.** So if
you pay back a big chunk, the remaining smaller amount is what keeps earning interest from then on.

> **Example:** You borrow ₹50,000 and keep it for 2 months and 25 days. You pay 1% for each of the
> 2 months, plus a small amount for the 25 extra days. If you then pay back ₹20,000, only the
> remaining ₹30,000 keeps building interest until you clear it.

### 5. If a loan runs late (overdue)

If a loan isn't paid back within 5 months, the app marks it as **overdue** so everyone can see it.
The loan stays active — it isn't cancelled. Right now there's **no extra charge** for being
overdue; it's just clearly flagged. (The club *can* switch on an extra charge later if it wants.)

### 6. Growing the money outside the club

Besides lending to members, the club grows money in two outside ways:

- **General places (like a bank).** The club puts money somewhere safe that pays a return — for
  example, a member keeps spare club cash in a bank and the bank pays some interest, which comes
  back to the club as earnings.
- **Chit funds.** This is a popular savings-and-borrowing scheme. The club **pays a set amount
  every month for a fixed number of months** and **receives a lump sum** at some point. Even if the
  club takes its lump sum early, it must **keep paying the monthly amounts until the scheme ends** —
  so the app remembers that future commitment too.

Anything the club gets back **above what it put in** is **profit**.

### 7. How the club earns and shares profit

The club's earnings come from **two places**: the **interest members pay on loans**, and the
**returns from outside investments** (bank, chit funds).

A few simple points about profit:

- Interest that has built up on current loans but isn't collected yet **still counts as earnings**
  (the club will collect it).
- **Monthly savings are not "profit"** — that's just each member's own money working in the club.
- Money the club still has to pay into chit funds is **set aside** first, so the club never claims
  more profit than it truly has.

The app then shows a friendly headline number: **profit per member** — the shareable profit divided
by the number of members.

**For now, profit isn't paid out regularly.** It keeps growing as each member's share, and a member
**receives their share when they leave** (a yearly payout could be added in the future).

**Profit is shared by how much you've actually paid in.** If you've kept up fully with your monthly
savings, you get a full share. If you're behind, you get a proportionally smaller share — that's
the fair trade-off. The app shows you both: the full share you *could* have, and your actual share.

### 8. Leaving the club — and coming back

A member can leave whenever they like. Leaving means **taking your money out and settling up**:
your savings, **plus your fair share of profit**, **minus** anything you still owe on a loan.

- It's **all or nothing** — you can't take out just a part or just the profit while staying in.
- The app calculates a suggested settlement, and the manager confirms the final amount.
- Once you leave, your share resets to zero and your **membership is closed** — but **your full
  history is kept**, nothing is erased.

**It works like a bank.** You're always the same person (one login), but **each time you're in the
club is a separate "membership," like a bank account that opens when you join and closes when you
leave.** If you **come back later**, the club **opens a fresh membership** for you — you pay your back
deposits plus a **catch-up** to be equal again — and your old membership stays on file as history. So
your new chapter starts clean, with the old one still there to look back on.

### 9. Late monthly savings & unpaid interest

If you pay your monthly savings late, the app shows it as **pending / overdue** so it's visible.

The club can also switch on **two automatic penalties** (the managers choose the amounts and the
date they start from — both are on today):

- **Falling behind on savings** — if your unpaid savings stay high (more than about three months'
  worth), a small monthly charge (e.g. 2%) is added until you catch up.
- **Not clearing loan interest** — after you finish paying back a loan, you get a grace month to
  clear the interest; if it's still unpaid, a small charge (e.g. 2% of the *interest*, never the loan
  itself) is added each month until it's paid.

These are added **automatically**, each one clearly labelled with **what it was for and the exact
sums** — e.g. *"Deposit penalty · Jul 2026 — 2% of ₹15,000 deposit pending"* — so you can always see
how a charge was worked out. The managers can see the full list on an **Auto penalties** page (and
filter it by type). Nothing is ever charged twice, and a charge is never on top of another charge —
always just on what you still owe.

### 10. Everyone can see everything

Honesty is the heart of the club. **Every member can see the full picture at any time** — all
members, all loans, all outside investments, every transaction, and their own personal summary.
Members can view everything; only managers can make changes. Anyone can also **download the
transaction list as a spreadsheet** to keep their own copy.

The app also gives friendly nudges: a few days before month-end it drops a **reminder in the app**
for anyone whose monthly savings are behind, so nobody gets a penalty by simply forgetting. And
once a month it **emails a full copy of the club's records to the manager** for safekeeping.

### 11. Ask the club on WhatsApp

Members can also just **message the club's WhatsApp number** — no login, no app. The club
recognises you by your phone number. Send *balance* and it replies with what you've saved and
what you're owed; *loan* shows your loan and interest; *due* shows anything pending. Managers can
also ask about any member, and even record a payment by typing something like *"Ravi paid 2000"*
— the bot shows a preview and asks them to tap **Confirm** before anything is written down.
Nothing goes into the books without that confirmation.

You can write it the natural way: *"cibi loan 100000 for swathis on 01/12/2026"* works just as well
as *"…to swathis on 2026-12-01"* — the words for/to/from, everyday amounts like *1L*, and dates in
any common format are all understood. **Send a photo with the message** (as its caption) and the
picture is kept with that transaction as proof; managers can also add or change a transaction's
photo on the website.

**Forgot your password? WhatsApp sorts it out too.** The login screen has two shortcuts that open a
chat with the club's WhatsApp number:

- Send *quick login* and the bot replies with a **one-tap link** — open it and you're straight into
  the app, **no password needed** (the link works once and only for a few minutes).
- Send *reset password* and the bot **sets your password back to your phone number**, so you can sign
  in and pick a new one.

It's safe because WhatsApp already knows it's really your phone — the club only ever helps the number
it recognises as yours. And you always start the chat yourself; the club never messages you first.

Managers get one more view: a **WhatsApp usage** page showing who's actually using the assistant,
who hasn't tried it yet, and any unknown numbers that have messaged the club — and they can open any
member's chat history for a chosen day.

### 12. Mistakes are fixed honestly

People make typos. When something is entered wrong, it's **never quietly deleted**. Instead a
correction is added that cancels the mistake (and the right entry is put in), so the original stays
on record. Every change is logged. This keeps the club's books trustworthy.

## A quick story

To tie it together:

- **Asha** pays her ₹2,000 for the month to **Ravi**, who is holding club cash this month.
- **Bhaskar** borrows ₹2,50,000 — half from Ravi today, the rest from **Meera** a week later. He
  pays 1% monthly interest on what he owes, day by day.
- The club puts some money into a **chit fund** and keeps a little in a **bank** that pays interest.
- The **app's home screen** shows how much cash the club has (and who's holding it), how much is out
  on loan, what's invested outside, and the **profit per member**.
- **Priya** decides to leave. She'd paid two-thirds of her expected savings, so she gets two-thirds
  of a full profit share, settles up, and her account is paused — with all her history kept. Months
  later she **rejoins** by paying back in plus a catch-up, and she's equal again.

That's Peacock Investment Club: **many feathers, one fortune** — everyone saving together, helping
each other, and growing a shared treasure, fairly and openly.
